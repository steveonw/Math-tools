"That which is in locomotion must arrive at the half-way stage before it arrives at the goal."
— Aristotle, describing Zeno's paradox
The Resolution: The infinite series ½ + ¼ + ⅛ + ... = Σ(½)ⁿ converges to exactly 1.
The "paradox" assumes infinite steps require infinite time — but if each step takes proportionally less time, the total time is finite!

Geometric Series Formula: Σ arⁿ = a/(1-r) when |r| < 1
